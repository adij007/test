Advanced Mathematics: Integrals

Definite and Indefinite Integrals

An integral represents accumulation, such as area under a curve. The general form is:

\[ \int_a^b f(x)\,dx = F(b) - F(a) \]

where \( F(x) \) is the antiderivative of \( f(x) \).

Integration techniques include:

  • Integration by Parts: \( \int u\,dv = uv - \int v\,du \)
  • Substitution: \( \int f(g(x))g'(x)\,dx = \int f(u)\,du \)
  • Partial Fractions and Trigonometric Integrals

Physics: Special Relativity

Key Concepts

Special relativity revolutionized space-time concepts. Core equations include:

\[ E = mc^2 \]

and time dilation:

\[ \Delta t' = \frac{\Delta t}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}} \]
